INSTRUCTIONS

Hello!

Your task is to work your way through each of the 3 Case Studies to earn your Air Canada Certified Expert (ACE) designation. These Case Studies are scenarios to help you practice what you have learned so far. Each case study has a time limit of 45 minutes and you can keep track of time with the countdown clock in the upper left corner of the screen. Your score appears below the countdown clock. 10 points will be earned for each correct response.

Each case study is comprised of six scenarios, with three multiple choice questions in each; a total of 18 questions in each case study. You will need to earn 80% to pass (earning at least 150 of 180 possible points). In the event you don’t correctly answer a question on your first attempt, you will be provided with an alternate question on the same topic, giving you a second chance at getting it right and earning your 10 points. Alternate questions will only be displayed when an incorrect answer has been provided.

In this first lobby scene, click and read all of the RED or RED outlined items. Note, you must click on all the RED elements in any scene to move on. Once you have completed an item a green checkmark will appear to indicate that it has been completed.

Select the case study you wish to complete from the list on the white board next to the door. A reminder, in order to proceed to the case study, you must have viewed all mandatory items outlined in red. The online e-brochures on the rack next to the desk are for your reference and are not mandatory.

Within each case study, clicking on the RED highlighted items in the office, (e.g. smartphone, mouse, phone, poster, video monitor, etc.), will reveal the case study scenarios. You are not required start the scenarios in any order, however, please note all answers are final.

You can access these instructions at any time, from the link available at the bottom of every scene.

For each scenario presented you will be asked to select the correct answer(s) from the options provided. A correct answer will earn you 10 points. You can return to review previously answered questions, however, you will be unable to revise your responses.

Once you complete the first case study you will be prompted to begin the next. You are however not required to complete all three case studies in one session. The system will remember the case studies that you have already completed and passed. Successfully completing all case studies by December 31st, 2016 will earn you your 2017 Air Canada Expert (ACE) certification.

Here are the features, controls, and policies that will guide your online learning experience:

  • Interactive case study. To enhance the learning experience we have designed this Case Study to include text, graphics, and audio narration. This Case Study also incorporates interactivity features throughout. Each page will contain page or media controls to help you navigate easily.
  • RED items: all red items in any scene must be completed before the system will allow you to proceed.
  • Passing Score: Each Case Study has a time limit of 45 minutes. 10 points will be earned for correct responses. There are a total of 180 points available. You will need to earn 80% to pass (earning at least 150 of 180 possible points). In the event you don’t correctly answer a question on your first attempt, you will be provided with an alternate question on the same topic, giving you a second chance at getting it right and earning your 10 points. Alternate questions will only be displayed when an incorrect answer has been provided. The clock and score are always visible in the top left corner of any scene.
  • Attempts: 2 per case study, 6 in total
  • Completion: Upon the successful completion of each case study, you will receive an email confirming your score. Upon the successful completion of the three case studies you will receive confirmation of your ACE Designation and ACE certificate of completion.
  • Support: The system will detect and advise automatically if you are using unrecommended software or devises to access these case studies. If you have any technical issues or questions please contact support@learninglibrary.com or if you have any questions relating to the case study content, please contact Air Canada Globals Sales University team directly at acglobalsalestraining@aircanada.ca.

INTRO

Welcome to the Air Canada Global Sales University.

Training is without question – a key metric of success in our globalized world. At Air Canada, we have invested heavily in training in everything from customer soft skills to sophisticated training equipment to teach pilots to fly our newest technology aircraft.

The Global Sales Team at Air Canada is also focused on developing training opportunities that allow our travel partners to build the skills and knowledge needed to be successful.

In a competitive environment such as ours, training becomes even more important as a tool to help our partners become Air Canada Brand Ambassadors. Customers have a lot of questions and we know counselors do too. We want to ensure you and your team can answer them confidently. That is why the Global Sales team is committed to providing our partners with the tools that build on the extensive skill set and knowledge that counselors have already acquired.

These case studies, and the content shared within the learning modules of the Air Canada Global Sales University demonstrate our commitment to training and skills development. Helping our partners become aware of our extensive product offering allow us to jointly serve our customers, offering them value and choice.

We applaud and appreciate your personal investment in this training program.
